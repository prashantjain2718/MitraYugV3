Receiving Phone Calls

Learn how to answer incoming calls on your smartphone. This guide will help you handle calls from friends and family.

Video tutorial will be available soon

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

When Someone Calls

Your phone will ring and show a screen with the caller's name or number. You'll see two buttons: a green one to answer and a red one to decline.

2

Answer the Call

To answer the call, swipe the green button towards the right or tap it. This will connect you to the caller.

3

During the Call

You'll see options like speaker, mute, and end call. The speaker button makes the call louder, and mute turns off your microphone.

4

End the Call

When you're done talking, tap the red phone button to end the call. This button is usually at the bottom of the screen.

Helpful Tips

  • If you're busy, you can swipe the red button to decline the call
  • Use the speaker button if you have trouble hearing
  • The mute button is useful when you need to talk to someone else briefly
  • You can see missed calls in your phone app's recent calls list

Common Issues and Solutions

Can't Hear the Caller

Try using the speaker button or adjusting your phone's volume using the side buttons.

Accidentally Declined a Call

You can call back by finding the number in your recent calls list and tapping it.

Call Dropped

Check your network signal and try calling back. If it happens often, you might need to move to an area with better coverage.
